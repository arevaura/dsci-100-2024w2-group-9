1. Data Description:

The players.csv dataset includes a total of 196 observations and 7 variables. The variables include: experience (Categorical: Pro, Veteran, Amateur, Regular)

  1. subscribe (Boolean: True/False)
  2. hashedEmail (String: Unique player identifier)
  3. played_hours (Float: Total hours played ranging from 0 to 223.1)
  4. name (String: Player's name)
  5. gender (String: Male/Female)
  6. Age (Integer: Age in years ranging from 9 to 50)

Some potential issues include missing values in the Age column and there may be a bias as players who engage more would be overrepresented. Data is most likely collected from gaming platforms that track player activity and subscription rates.

The sessions.csv dataset includes 1535 observations and 5 variables. The variables include:

  1. hashedEmail (String: Unique player identifier)
  2. start_time (String: Timestamps for session ranging from January 5, 2024, 00:58 AM to December 9, 2024, 02:47 AM)
  3. end_time (String: Timestamps for session ranging from January 5, 2024, 01:24 AM to December 9, 2024, 03:04 AM)
  4. Original_start_time (Float: Unix timestamps)
  5. original_end_time (Float: Unix timestamps) A potential issue is missing values in end_time. Also, sessions without end_time make it impossible to calculate duration and players without recorded sessions need to be accounted for. Data is most likely collected from game server logs while missing session data for some players could indicate technical errors or inactivity.

Sessions dataset will not be used as it doesnt include relevant information to address my question.

2. Questions

The broader question I am addressing is Question 1: What player characteristics and behaviours are most predictive of subscribing to a game-related newsletter, and how do these features differ between various player types? The specific question is: Can a players experience level and played_hours predict whether they are subscribed and how do these features differ by age? This investigates the characteristics, experience and hours played to determine which are most likely to subscribe and how these trends differ by age groups. For example, players with Pro experience level averaging 20 hours played may be more likely to be subscribed in the age group 9-18 compared to in the age group 30-40, which may have higher likelihood of subscription with experience level Regular and averaging 5 hours played.

4. Methods and Plan

The method being used is K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN) which is appropriate because it works well when the data is non-linear as it doesnt assume any specific data distribution, making it a flexible model. The dataset will be split before training into 80% training and 20% testing, as we want to maximize model learning while still having enough data to test on. K-Fold (5 fold) Cross-Validation will be used to determine the optimal value of k in KNN which helps to reduce variance in model performance. The model will be evaluated, compared and selected using accuracy, precision and recall. A possible limitation is that outliers can affect results. However, the impact of outliers is reduced by scaling/standardizing the columns played_hours and Age to ensure fair calculations with a mean of 0 and standard deviation of 1.
